Choosing a reel is a process. There are differents styles and sizes of reels that are best suited for certain applications. There are two main styles of reels in the bass fishing world. Low Profile baitcasters (on left) and Spinning reels (on right) are the two major styles used in the bass fishing world. See the table below to see the short version of each reel design.

A quick look at the advantages/disadvantages of each reel design

Baitcasting Spinning
Quality models begin at around $100 Quality models begin at around $50
Handles heavy line better due to light line causing digging Better with light line due to lack of digging
Better with high memory line High memory line causes knots
Has a steap learning curve to prevent backlashing Can be cast with virtually no training
Rods designed for baitcasting reels are generally for heavier lure weights Rods designed for nearly all lure weights
